How it Began

The history behind Toblerone begins with Swiss chocolatier, Jean Tobler. In 1868 he opened his own confectionery shop in Bern, and after years of mastering the art and craft of creating high quality, unique tasting chocolates, Tobler was able to open up his own chocolate factory.

In 1900 his son Theodor Tobler took over the thriving business, and in 1908 he and his cousin Emil Baumann created the triangular chocolate bar with honey and almond nougat, that we today know as Toblerone. The confectionery has since become Switzerland's most iconic chocolate.
